# Example Self-Rally Script

**Duration**: 5 minutes
**Mode**: Energizing (Self-Rally Arc)
**Target**: Entrepreneur preparing for investor pitch, feeling imposter syndrome
**Word Count**: ~850 words (170 WPM)
**Questionnaire Data Used**: innerCritic, pastSuccess, victoryVision, identityStatement, physicalCue

> **Architecture shift (2026-02):** This example follows the Self-Rally Arc (Spiral → Confrontation → Reframe → Fuel → Lock-In) for solo listeners. No "we/us/together" language. Pronoun arc: I → You → Imperative.

---

## Full Script Text

### Beat 1: THE SPIRAL (first-person adjacent)

I know what's happening right now.

That tightness in your chest. That voice — the one that sounds reasonable, the one that sounds like it's trying to protect you — it's saying "Who are you to walk in there and ask for millions?"

It's saying you're not ready. It's saying they'll see right through you. It's saying everyone else in that room has done this a hundred times, and you — you're just pretending.

I know. I hear it too.

And right now, sitting with that feeling, it's easy to believe the voice is right.

### Beat 2: THE CONFRONTATION (shift to direct second-person)

But let me ask you something. [intense]

Is that who you are?

Is the person sitting here doubting themselves the same person who quit a six-figure job because they believed in something bigger? Is that the person who built this from nothing — from a notebook sketch and a maxed-out credit card?

No.

That voice isn't protecting you. It's lying to you. And you know it's lying because you've heard it before — before every pitch, before every hard conversation, before every moment that mattered — and every single time, you showed up anyway.

So here's the truth: You're not nervous because you're not ready. You're nervous because this matters. And things that matter are supposed to feel like this.

### Beat 3: THE REFRAME (second-person intimate)

[confident] You are someone who builds things that didn't exist before.

That's not a motivational quote. That's a fact. Look at what you've done. Eighteen months ago this company was an idea. Today it has customers. It has revenue. It has a team that chose to bet on YOUR vision — not because you sold them on it, but because they felt it.

You didn't get here by accident. Every late night debugging the product. Every "no" from the first forty investors. Every time you rewrote the pitch deck at 2am because the story wasn't right yet — that wasn't desperation. That was mastery being built, rep by rep, in real time.

This is YOUR choice. Nobody put you in that room. You chose this. You chose it because building things that matter is who you are — not what you do, who you ARE.

And the people across that table? They're not gatekeepers. They're looking for exactly what you have — conviction backed by evidence, vision grounded in execution. You're not asking them for charity. You're offering them a seat on something real.

### Beat 4: THE FUEL (second-person + sensory peak)

[passionate] Now picture this.

Two hours from now. You're walking out of that conference room. Your phone is buzzing. Your co-founder is texting "How'd it go?" and you're typing back one word — "Yes."

Feel that. Feel your shoulders drop. Feel that exhale — the one where your whole body lets go because you KNOW. Not hope. Not think. KNOW.

You walk to your car and you sit there for a second. Just a second. And in that silence you feel something you haven't felt in months — that this is working. That the thing you imagined in that notebook is becoming real. That every sacrifice had a point.

Remember the last time you felt that? [excited] Remember the first customer who said "This changed everything for me"? Remember how you read that message three times because you couldn't believe it was real?

That feeling. That exact feeling. It's waiting for you on the other side of the next sixty minutes.

That voice from before — the one saying you're not ready? It's quiet now. Because you gave it something it couldn't argue with. Evidence.

You've done this. You've done harder than this. You've done the version of this that had no proof, no traction, no team. And NOW you have all three.

### Beat 5: THE LOCK-IN (imperative commands)

[determined] So here's what happens next.

You stand up.

You take one breath — deep, through the nose, slow through the mouth.

You roll your shoulders back. Drop the tension. Feel your feet on the ground.

Now clench your fist once — hard — and release it.

That's the switch. That's you telling your body what your mind already knows.

You're not walking into that room hoping it goes well. You're walking in there to show them what you've built and let the work speak for itself.

One meeting. One story. YOUR story.

Go tell it.

## What This Script Does NOT Do

- Does NOT use "we/us/together" — the listener is alone
- Does NOT sound like a coach addressing a team
- Does NOT use polished anaphora ("We are the ones who...")
- Does NOT use calming language ("allow yourself," "gently notice")
- Does NOT give multiple action steps — ONE physical sequence at the end
- Does NOT use generic imagery — every detail maps to the user's specific context

## Comparison: Old Group-Speech vs. New Self-Rally

| Dimension | Old (Group Speech) | New (Self-Rally) |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Opening | "Right now, in this moment..." (urgency) | "I know what's happening right now..." (recognition) |
| Identity | "We are the ones who..." (tribal) | "You are someone who..." (personal) |
| Tension | "Saying isn't the same as DOING" (generic) | "Is that who you are?" (confrontation) |
| Imagery | "Picture this with me..." (shared) | "Two hours from now, you're walking out..." (intimate) |
| Ending | "Go show them who we are" (collective) | "Go tell it." (singular, imperative) |
