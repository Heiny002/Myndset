## Academic Sources

### Primary Source: Enhancing Imagery through Hypnosis: A Performance Aid for Athletes
- **Authors**: Liggett, D. R.
- **Year**: 2000
- **Title**: Enhancing Imagery through Hypnosis: A Performance Aid for Athletes
- **Journal**: American Journal of Clinical Hypnosis, 43(2), 149-157
- **URL**: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00029157.2000.10404267
- **Key Findings**: Using Martens' Sport Imagery Questionnaire, 14 athletes imaged four different sport situations both in and out of hypnosis. Participants reported that imagery under hypnosis was significantly more intense across all four dimensions: visual, auditory, kinesthetic, and affective. Imagery was also more intense for each sport situation tested (practicing alone, practicing in front of others, watching a teammate, competing). The findings demonstrate that hypnosis substantially enhances imagery vividness and effectiveness, making it a powerful adjunct to mental rehearsal for athletic performance.
- **Effect Size**: Significant increases in imagery intensity across all four sensory dimensions under hypnosis vs. waking state

### Supporting Studies

**Assessing the Immediate and Maintained Effects of Hypnosis on Self-Efficacy and Soccer Wall-Volley Performance (2010)**
- **Authors**: Barker, J. B., Jones, M. V., & Greenlees, I.
- **Year**: 2010
- **Title**: Assessing the Immediate and Maintained Effects of Hypnosis on Self-Efficacy and Soccer Wall-Volley Performance
- **Journal**: Journal of Sport and Exercise Psychology, 32(2), 243-252
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20479480/
- **Key Findings**: Fifty-nine collegiate soccer players were randomly allocated to either a hypnosis group (n=30) or video attention-control group (n=29). The hypnotic intervention comprised three sessions using ego-strengthening suggestions and mental rehearsal. Results indicated that the hypnosis group showed significantly higher self-efficacy and better wall-volley performance than the control group, both immediately following intervention and at 4-week follow-up. This demonstrates that hypnosis-enhanced mental rehearsal produces lasting performance improvements.
- **Effect Size**: Significant group differences maintained at 4-week follow-up; specific d not reported but statistically significant (p < .05)

**Effects of Hypnosis on Flow States and Golf Performance (2001)**
- **Authors**: Pates, J., Oliver, R., & Maynard, I.
- **Year**: 2001
- **Title**: Effects of Hypnosis on Flow States and Golf Performance
- **Journal**: Perceptual and Motor Skills, 91(3), 1057-1075
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11219648/
- **Key Findings**: Investigated the effects of a hypnosis intervention on flow states and golf putting performance. The hypnotic intervention included a deep relaxation induction phase, a hypnotic regression phase to relive optimal performance experiences, and a trigger control phase to bring ideal performance state under voluntary control. All participants showed increases in both flow state scores and golf putting performance from baseline to intervention. Participants reported feeling more confident, relaxed, and calm.
- **Effect Size**: Positive improvements in both flow and performance across all participants

**Meta-Analysis: Effects of Mental Preparation Techniques Based on Imagery-Hypnosis on Flow States and Athletic Performance (2020)**
- **Authors**: Hernandez-Mendo, A., et al.
- **Year**: 2020
- **Title**: Meta-Analysis: Effects of Mental Preparation Techniques Based on Imagery-Hypnosis on Flow States and Athletic Performance
- **Journal**: Revista MHSalud, 17(1), 31-60
- **URL**: https://www.scielo.sa.cr/scielo.php?pid=S1659-44362020000100031&script=sci_abstract
- **Key Findings**: Meta-analysis examining 17 studies on the effectiveness of imagery and hypnosis-based mental preparation techniques on flow states and sports performance. Found a positive effect size for flow state (ES = .782) and for performance (ES = .339). The combined use of imagery and hypnosis showed moderate to strong positive effects on both psychological state and objective performance outcomes.
- **Effect Size**: Flow state ES = .782 (large); Performance ES = .339 (small-to-medium)

**Does Mental Practice Enhance Performance? A Meta-Analysis (1994)**
- **Authors**: Driskell, J. E., Copper, C., & Moran, A.
- **Year**: 1994
- **Title**: Does Mental Practice Enhance Performance?
- **Journal**: Journal of Applied Psychology, 79(4), 481-492
- **URL**: https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1995-00363-001
- **Key Findings**: Foundational meta-analysis establishing that mental practice has a positive and significant effect on performance. Effect was moderated by task type (larger for cognitive vs. purely physical tasks), retention interval, and practice duration. Mental practice was especially effective for tasks with large cognitive load demands. This meta-analysis provides the evidence base for mental rehearsal as a performance enhancement technique, which hypnosis amplifies through enhanced imagery vividness.
- **Effect Size**: Overall r = .255, d = .527 (medium effect)

**The Application of Hypnosis in Sports (2022)**
- **Authors**: Wang, Y., Chen, C., & Xu, Y.
- **Year**: 2022
- **Title**: The Application of Hypnosis in Sports
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Psychology, 12, 771162
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8820393/
- **Key Findings**: Comprehensive review concluding that hypnosis helps athletes improve psychological quality, increase self-confidence, relieve fatigue, restore energy, concentrate better during training and competition, and improve skills. The review identifies mental rehearsal under hypnosis as a primary mechanism through which sport hypnosis operates, noting that trance-enhanced visualization allows athletes to rehearse performance with heightened sensory clarity.
- **Effect Size**: Narrative review; references multiple studies showing positive effects

## Implementation Protocol

### Clinical Guidelines
1. Mental rehearsal under hypnosis should be conducted with athletes/performers who are motivated and have clear performance goals
2. Pre-session interview should identify the specific performance scenario to rehearse in vivid detail
3. Hypnotic induction should achieve at least a medium trance depth before beginning rehearsal
4. Rehearsal should be from an associated perspective (first-person, through own eyes) for maximum motor cortex activation
5. All sensory modalities should be engaged: visual, auditory, kinesthetic, olfactory, and emotional
6. The rehearsed scenario should be realistic and achievable to maintain credibility with the unconscious mind
7. Post-rehearsal anchoring should link the rehearsed state to a performance trigger

### Step-by-Step Process

**Phase 1: Hypnotic Induction and Deepening (2-3 minutes)**
1. Use progressive relaxation or Elman-style rapid induction
2. Deepen with fractionation, counting, or staircase deepener
3. Establish ideomotor signals if desired (finger lift for "yes")
4. Verify adequate trance depth through observable indicators (muscle relaxation, breathing pattern, REM)
5. Install a "control room" or "inner screen" metaphor for accessing imagery

**Phase 2: Optimal Performance State Access (1-2 minutes)**
1. Guide the client to recall a specific time of peak performance
2. "Go back to that time when everything came together perfectly..."
3. Amplify the memory: what they saw, heard, felt physically and emotionally
4. Stack the kinesthetic sensations: confidence, flow, power, precision
5. Anchor this peak state with a physical trigger (fist clench, finger press)

**Phase 3: Future Performance Rehearsal (3-5 minutes)**
1. Transition from past peak state to the upcoming performance scenario
2. "Now, carrying all those feelings with you, project forward to [specific event]..."
3. Guide vivid, first-person imagery of the entire performance sequence
4. Include pre-performance routine, warm-up, and competition environment
5. Rehearse specific skills, movements, and decision points in real time
6. Include handling of adversity moments (recovering from setbacks, maintaining composure)
7. Build to the climactic moment of successful execution
8. Experience the emotional reward of successful performance

**Phase 4: Reinforcement and Generalization (1-2 minutes)**
1. Replay the successful performance at accelerated speed (mental fast-forward)
2. Install post-hypnotic suggestion: "Every time you [trigger], you automatically access this state"
3. Future pace: "And in the days leading up to [event], this rehearsal replays in your mind..."
4. Seal the suggestion: "Your unconscious mind has now recorded this performance blueprint..."

**Phase 5: Emergence and Integration (1 minute)**
1. Standard emergence procedure with positive suggestions
2. "Bringing all those resources with you as you return to full awareness..."
3. Brief conscious debrief to reinforce the experience
4. Assign self-practice homework (replay rehearsal mentally before sleep)

### Contraindications
- Active psychosis or severe dissociative disorders
- Individuals with a history of traumatic experiences in the performance domain (e.g., severe sports injury PTSD) without appropriate clinical support
- Untrained practitioners attempting deep hypnotic work
- Performance anxiety rooted in legitimate safety concerns (should address root cause first)
- Individuals who are deeply resistant to or fearful of hypnosis

### Cautions
- Imagery vividness varies significantly across individuals; some may need imagery training before hypnotic rehearsal
- Rehearsing unrealistic outcomes can undermine confidence when reality differs
- Over-reliance on mental rehearsal without physical practice is counterproductive
- Some athletes may experience increased anxiety if rehearsal triggers performance pressure
- Hypnotic suggestibility varies; assess before promising specific outcomes
- Rehearsal should complement, not replace, physical training and skill development
- Monitor for perfectionism tendencies that may create rigid expectations

## Practitioner Scripts & Language Patterns

### Example Script Excerpt
"And now, with your eyes closed and your body deeply relaxed, I want you to project forward to race day. You're there now, standing at the starting line. Feel the ground beneath your feet. Hear the crowd around you. Notice the air on your skin. And as the starter prepares to call, you feel that surge of calm power flowing through every muscle. Your body knows exactly what to do. You've trained for this moment. And when the signal sounds, you explode forward with perfect form, every stride powerful and precise, your breathing rhythmic and strong. You're in the zone now, moving with that effortless flow you've felt before. The competition fades away and there is only you and the perfect execution of everything you've practiced. Feel yourself crossing the finish line, the rush of accomplishment, knowing you've performed at your absolute best..."

### Specific Language Patterns

1. **Temporal Projection**: "Project yourself forward now to [specific event]... you're there, right now, in that moment..."
2. **Sensory Amplification**: "Notice every detail... what you see becomes clearer and brighter... the sounds become more vivid... the feelings in your body intensify..."
3. **Kinesthetic Activation**: "Feel your muscles firing in perfect sequence... the power flowing through your legs... your grip firm and sure..."
4. **Identity Pacing**: "This is who you are... a prepared, focused, powerful performer... and your body knows it..."
5. **Dissociation-Association Shift**: "Watch yourself performing perfectly from the outside... now step INTO that image... see through your own eyes... feel everything..."
6. **Process Language**: "Everything flowing naturally... each movement connected to the next... your training taking over automatically..."
7. **Confidence Installation**: "You've done this thousands of times... your body remembers... and it remembers now..."
8. **Adversity Inoculation**: "And if anything unexpected happens, you adjust instantly, effortlessly... staying centered, staying strong..."
9. **Temporal Compression**: "And now, fast-forward through the entire performance at triple speed, feeling every perfect moment compressed into seconds..."
10. **Post-Hypnotic Rehearsal Trigger**: "And tonight, as you drift off to sleep, this mental movie replays automatically, programming your performance even deeper..."

### Milton Model Elements
- **Process Instructions**: "Allow the images to form naturally... let them become clearer in their own time..."
- **Presuppositions**: "As your performance continues to improve..." (presupposes improvement)
- **Temporal Predicates**: "Begin to see yourself... continue to feel... as you move through..."
- **Embedded Commands**: "You can PERFORM WITH CONFIDENCE as you rehearse this now..."
- **Truisms**: "Every elite athlete uses mental rehearsal... and you're doing it now..."
- **Mind Reading**: "And part of you already knows exactly how this performance unfolds..."
- **Cause-Effect**: "The more vividly you see it, the more powerfully your body responds..."
- **Selectional Restriction Violation**: "And your muscles remember the choreography of success..."
- **Ambiguity**: "And your performance... continues to develop... in ways that surprise you..."

## Delivery Notes
- **Tone**: Confident, steady, and progressively energizing as the rehearsal builds toward peak performance
- **Pacing**: Slow and immersive during sensory setup; match the natural tempo of the performance during rehearsal (faster for sprints, rhythmic for distance events, deliberate for precision sports)
- **Volume**: Moderate during setup; may increase slightly during peak performance moments to match intensity
- **Emphasis**: Strong emphasis on kinesthetic and sensory words; mark embedded commands with tonal shifts
- **Pauses**: 2-3 second pauses during sensory descriptions to allow imagery formation; shorter pauses during active performance sequences to maintain flow
- **Duration**: Full session 15-25 minutes; core rehearsal segment 3-5 minutes; brief reinforcement version 4 minutes
- **Energy**: Start calm and grounded; build energy progressively to match the performance intensity being rehearsed; return to calm during consolidation
- **Repetition**: Replay key performance moments 2-3 times with increasing speed and certainty

## Evidence Level Assessment
- **Rating**: Moderate
- **Rationale**: Mental rehearsal under hypnosis combines two evidence-based approaches: mental imagery/practice (d = .527, Driskell et al., 1994) and clinical hypnosis. Liggett (2000) demonstrated that hypnosis significantly enhances imagery vividness across all sensory modalities, providing a clear mechanism for why hypnosis-enhanced rehearsal outperforms standard mental practice. Barker et al. (2010) showed maintained performance improvements at 4-week follow-up in a randomized controlled trial. Meta-analytic evidence (Hernandez-Mendo et al., 2020) shows effect sizes of .782 for flow states and .339 for performance when imagery-hypnosis techniques are combined.

**Strengths**:
1. Mental practice alone has strong meta-analytic support (d = .527)
2. Hypnosis demonstrably enhances imagery vividness (Liggett, 2000)
3. RCT evidence shows maintained performance gains (Barker et al., 2010)
4. Meta-analysis of combined imagery-hypnosis shows positive effects on flow and performance
5. Neuroscience research confirms motor cortex activation during mental rehearsal
6. Widely used by elite athletes, military, and performing artists
7. Multiple converging lines of evidence across different study designs

**Limitations**:
1. Many sport hypnosis studies use small samples or single-subject designs
2. Difficult to isolate hypnosis contribution from mental rehearsal alone
3. Individual differences in hypnotic suggestibility and imagery ability affect outcomes
4. Limited research on optimal dosage and frequency for different performance domains
5. Practitioner skill and rapport quality introduce variability

## Additional Notes

### Neuroscience of Mental Rehearsal
- Functional MRI studies show that mental rehearsal activates the same motor cortex areas as physical execution, though at lower intensity
- The premotor cortex, supplementary motor area, and cerebellum are all engaged during vivid mental rehearsal
- Hypnosis appears to reduce competing neural activity (Oakley & Halligan, 2013), potentially allowing cleaner motor cortex activation during rehearsal
- Mirror neuron systems may be involved when rehearsing from a first-person perspective

### Optimal Practice Parameters
Based on meta-analytic evidence:
- **Session frequency**: 3 times per week for performance maintenance; daily before major competitions
- **Session duration**: 10-15 minutes of active rehearsal within a 20-25 minute hypnosis session
- **Course length**: Minimum 4 weeks for establishing robust neural pathways
- **Imagery perspective**: First-person (associated) for motor skill enhancement; third-person for technique correction

### Integration with Physical Training
- Mental rehearsal is most effective when combined with physical practice, not as a substitute
- Best used as a supplement: physical practice for motor learning, mental rehearsal for confidence and consistency
- Particularly valuable during injury recovery when physical practice is limited
- Can be used to rehearse situations that are difficult to recreate in training (championship pressure, crowd noise, adverse conditions)

### Self-Hypnosis Protocol
Athletes can be taught a brief self-hypnosis version:
1. Close eyes, take 3 deep breaths, count down from 10 to 1
2. Access peak performance memory (30 seconds)
3. Rehearse upcoming performance (2-3 minutes)
4. Count up from 1 to 5, open eyes
